What does this test measure?

In this test the task is to fill in the gaps in an equation with numbers so that the given result is correct. Therefore, the test measures basic numeracy on the basis of the four basic arithmetic operations. A conscientious handling with numbers and a confident mastery of the four basic arithmetic operations is vitally important in certain occupational areas.

How to interpret your result

Compared to other participants, it is much easier for you to handle the tasks in this test successfully.

What is your processing style like?

In addition to the performance score your processing style is measured. It indicates how many questions you completed in the default time (speed) and how many of those questions you answered correctly (accuracy). These two scores are compared with the scores of other participants as well. The optimal processing style is to complete as many questions as possible, as accurately as possible.

Improving your skills of quick and accurate calculation is not easy and requires commitment over a long period of time. Here are some tips to help you to improve your skills in this area:

  • Solve simple equations regularly in which the four basic arithmetic operations – addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division – occur. You can also think-out simple equations yourself (e.g. based on the pattern X + Y * Z = ?) or you could find similar number problems in a book of basic mathematics.
  • Calculate a running total of your shopping basket when you are shopping. Multiply the prices of things of which you want to buy more than one instead of adding the prices, e.g. 3 yogurts €0.59 each = 3 * €0.59€, not €0.59 + €0.59 + €0.59. This exercise trains your ability to calculate and enables you to check the end result at the checkout.
